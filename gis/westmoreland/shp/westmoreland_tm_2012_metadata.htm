Description:
Abstract:
The Virginia Institute of Marine Science published the first Tidal marsh Inventories for the Westmoreland County in 1978 using data collected in the 1970's. Using high resolution color infra-red imagery from 2009 a new Tidal Marsh Inventory has been developed for 2012. Marsh boundaries were generated using heads-up digitizing techniques at a scale of 1:1,000. Each marsh polygon was classified by morphologic type: fringe, extensive, embayed, or marsh island. Marshes were ground-truthed in the field where a community type index was assigned to each marsh based on plant community make-up.
Purpose:
To maintain and update an inventory of the distribution of tidal marshes for the Commonwealth of Virginia.

Completeness_Report:
All tidal marshes depicted in high resolution imagery are digitized. Seleted marshes are ground-truthed in the field to determine community type index and dominant species present. Using best professional judgement, the community indices may be interpolated among wetland polygons not ground-truthed. In some cases, marshes are unclassified and their areal exent not verified in the field.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The 2012 TMI (Tidal Marsh Inventory) shapefile was created by visual photo interpretation and heads up digitizing of recent (2009) high resolution (6") infrared aerial photography at a scale of 1:1000. This scale was picked as a compromise between taking full advantage of the resolution and time constraints for project development. Marsh information from earlier surveys and NWI was used to help identify narrow fringe marshes. This delineation was completed prior to the fieldwork and then corrected as noted in the field.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Wetlands Advisory Group (WAG) scientists with experience identifying tidal wetland plant species are responsible for TMI field data collection. Field observations of tidal marshes are conducted visually from a small research vessel with the asssistance of tidal marsh base maps developed in the lab and binoculars. The WAG scientist compares observed tidal marsh boundaries with the digitized polygons on the aerial photographs.
Field data sheets are used to record information on all of the mapped marsh polygons that are ground truthed as tidal marshes. New Marshes that are not delineated as numbered polygons are drawn on aerial photos to be digiitized in the laboratory. Each new marsh is assigned a unique New Marsh ID number using pre-established sequences for each scientist. Previously digitized polygons that are determined through observation not to be marshes are noted for deletion.
All observed tidal wetland plant species within each Marsh and New Marsh polygon are recorded on the field data sheets. The relative cover of each species is indicated by dominance (>50%), trace (<1%), or just a presence between 1-49% cover recorded. Tidal wetland field guides are used to assist with plant identification when necessary and photographs of unknown species are taken. Unknown plant species may be collected, labeled and returned to the lab for immediate identification using standard wetland plant identification keys. The general wetland community type code is also recorded based on species dominance using a pre-established list .
Field data is then entered into a project-specific attribute table for easy assimilation into a geo-referenced database. Quality assurance checks after data entry include looking for and fixing duplicate polygon numbers, deleting all Not Marsh polygons, correcting inconsistent community codes, and verifying that the field surveyed marsh polygons in the final maps have the correct, corresponding field data.
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
In the lab, corrected/annotated field maps and data sheets are used to correct the original delineation of tidal marshes. Here, marshes boundaries are added, deleted or shifted based on ground-truthing. All polygons that were observed in the field are coded to reflect they have been field verified.
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Field information pertaining to community type, species dominance and presence or absence of Phragmites australis are logged in excel using the same marsh id number assisgned in the digital marsh boundariy layer. These data are extracted from the excel database and joined using the ArcMap command Join Field to the tidal marsh shapefile using the marsh id as the common attribute.
